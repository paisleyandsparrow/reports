The Problem We Solved
Thousands of campaigns.
No way to accept them all.

Amazon's Creator Connections program lists hundreds of thousands of brand campaigns offering elevated commission rates — sometimes 10–50% vs. the standard 4%. But enrollment is manual, one campaign at a time. Most creators accept a fraction of what's available and leave the rest on the table.

Before Automation

Manual enrollment, limited coverage

  • Campaigns accepted one at a time
  • No way to keep up with new campaigns daily
  • Hundreds of thousands of opportunities missed
  • Elevated commission rates left unclaimed
  • Revenue from qualifying sales earned at standard rates only
After Automation

Every available campaign enrolled automatically

  • 575,465 campaigns accepted on day one (April 1st)
  • ~2,410 new campaigns added by Amazon every day
  • Runs on a schedule — automatically captures every new campaign
  • Elevated rates applied to every qualifying sale
  • 1453 brand new revenue streams active in 19 days
The Proof
How we isolated what the automation actually added

To make sure we weren't counting campaigns Jen had already enrolled in manually, we built a strict attribution model using her full two-year earnings history.

Attribution Methodology
  1. Downloaded Jen's complete Creator Connections earnings history — all of 2025 and Jan–Apr 2026
  2. Identified every campaign that generated even a single sale before April 1, 2026 — flagged as pre-existing (17,469 campaigns excluded)
  3. Any campaign generating revenue on or after April 1 with zero prior history = confidently attributed to the automation
  4. Calculated commission income, revenue, and delta vs. standard 4% rates on net-new campaigns only
